The honest one-pager: what LEO is for, what it was trained on, how well it does, and — just as importantly — where it shouldn't be trusted. Metrics fill live from the evaluation snapshot.

Intended use

Probabilistic early-warning for transactional banking-API estates. Emits failure probability at 1, 5 and 15 minutes ahead so an orchestration layer can retry, pre-warm or reroute before customer impact.

  • In scope: per-API risk scoring, proactive failover, capacity heads-up.
  • Out of scope: root-cause diagnosis, fraud detection, individual-transaction approval.

Limitations

  • h+15 coverage sits at ~89.98% — a hair under the 90% target; treat the longest horizon as advisory.
  • Synthetic augmentation may under-represent rare, novel failure modes.
  • Calibrated on this estate's distribution — re-calibrate before porting to a new API mix.
  • Latency cost: proactive routing adds ~0.11s avg vs the reactive baseline.

Maintenance & ethics

  • Self-healing: nightly KS drift test → conditional retrain (see Drift).
  • Monitoring: coverage SLO + error budget per horizon (see Reliability).
  • Human-in-the-loop: reroute is reversible and logged; no irreversible action is automated.
  • Fairness: scores per API endpoint, not per customer — no protected attributes used.
